 Praise for its gripping plot: 
Many readers praise 'Gone Girl' for its suspenseful and unpredictable storyline. The book is known for its numerous twists and turns, keeping readers on the edge of their seats until the very end.

 Fascination with the characters: 
The characters in 'Gone Girl,' particularly the central couple Amy and Nick Dunne, are often described as complex and morally ambiguous. Some readers find them compelling and enjoy the psychological depth that Flynn brings to their portrayal.

 Criticism of its dark themes: 
'Gone Girl' deals with themes such as manipulation, deceit, and toxic relationships, which can be unsettling for some readers. Critics argue that the book's portrayal of marriage and gender dynamics may be problematic or even harmful.

 Debate over the ending: 
The ending of 'Gone Girl' is controversial and divisive among readers. Some find it satisfying and thematically fitting, while others feel that it is abrupt or unsatisfactory.

 Appreciation for Flynn's writing style: 
Gillian Flynn's writing style, characterized by its sharp prose and keen psychological insights, is widely praised by many readers.
